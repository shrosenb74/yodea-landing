Shavuot celebrates something that happened 50 days after Passover. What is it?

AThe building of the Temple in Jerusalem
BThe birth of King David
CThe giving of the Torah at Mount Sinai
DThe end of the 40 years in the desert
Correct — Shavuot marks the moment God gave the Torah to the Jewish people at Mount Sinai. The word "Shavuot" means "weeks" — it comes exactly 7 weeks after Passover. There's a tradition of eating dairy on Shavuot — cheesecake, blintzes, you name it. Why?

ADairy was the only food available in the desert
BMoses's mother fed him dairy when he was born
CWhen the Israelites received the Torah, they hadn't yet learned the kosher laws for meat
DMilk symbolises the sweetness of the Torah
Correct — one of the main reasons given is that when the Israelites received the Torah at Sinai, they suddenly had new laws about how to slaughter and prepare meat. Their vessels weren't kosher yet. So they ate dairy instead. There are other explanations too — including that the Torah is compared to "milk and honey" — but the kosher reason is the most widely cited. The Book of Ruth is read on Shavuot every year. Why Ruth specifically?

ARuth was born on Shavuot
BRuth chose to accept the Torah and join the Jewish people — just as Jews do on Shavuot
CThe story takes place during the wheat harvest, which is when Shavuot falls
DRuth was Moses's sister
Both B and C are actually true — but B is the deeper reason. Ruth was a Moabite woman who chose to follow her mother-in-law Naomi back to Israel and convert to Judaism, famously saying "your people shall be my people." Her voluntary acceptance of Jewish identity mirrors the moment at Sinai when the entire Jewish people accepted the Torah. Also yes — the story happens during the barley and wheat harvest, which is exactly when Shavuot falls. Two reasons for the price of one. Actually both B and C are true — but B is the deeper reason.
